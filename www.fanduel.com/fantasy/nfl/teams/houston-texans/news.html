Ogbo Okoronkwo

DE / Houston Texans
Okoronkwo recorded one tackle and a pass defensed in a 32-31 win over the Colts in Week 18.

Analysis:

Okoronkwo recorded career highs in tackles (44) and sacks (five) in his first year with Houston after three seasons with the Rams. He played a full 17-game season after injuries limited him in Los Angeles. The full season of health and career-high production puts the 27-year-old linebacker in a good position as he heads into free agency.

Desmond King II

CB / Houston Texans
King recorded six tackles and returned one punt for 16 yards in a 32-31 victory over the Colts in Week 18.

Analysis:

King ranked fourth on the defense with 89 tackles and tied for the lead with 10 for a loss. The six-year veteran has put together two consecutive strong seasons for the Texans, including five interceptions. King averaged 9.4 punt-return yards on 33 chances and returned one kickoff for 50 yards. He has one year left on his deal and should be part of the Texans' secondary in 2023.

Christian Kirksey

LB / Houston Texans
Kirksey had five tackles, an interception and one pass defensed in a 32-31 win over Indianapolis in Week 18.

Analysis:

Kirksey rewarded the organization that signed him to a two-year deal prior to the season with his first triple-digit tackle count in nine NFL seasons. His 124 stops ranked third on the team. He also finished with two interceptions and a recovered fumble. The starting middle linebacker should return in 2023, although there could be changes to the defense with a new coaching regime.

Jalen Pitre

S / Houston Texans
Pitre (concussion) recorded eight tackles before leaving Houston's 32-31 win over the Colts in Week 18.

Analysis:

Pitre led the Texans with 147 tackles and five interceptions. The rookie second-round pick out of Baylor, who started the year strong but faded in the middle, resuscitated his season after being moved from strong to free safety. After the switch, Pitre recorded 71 tackles over the final six games of the season. Given health, Pitre will have a starting spot on the back end in 2023.

Brandin Cooks

WR / Houston Texans
Cooks told front office personnel that he does not want to be part of Houston's rebuilding process, Jonathan M. Alexander of the Houston Chronicle reports.

Analysis:

Cooks reiterated his desire to play for a team with a chance to compete for a championship, which is unlikely to happen any time soon in Houston. This should not come as a surprise, as Cooks expressed displeasure when he wasn't moved at the trade deadline. He has a massive cap hit in the final two years of his deal, so Houston will likely have to eat a portion of his salary in order to move him. That was an obstacle the Texans ran into at the deadline.
